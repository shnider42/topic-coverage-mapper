# Example Client Memo

# YouTube Landscape Scan: Young Adult Career Coaching / Mentoring

## Executive summary

The YouTube landscape for early-career advice appears crowded at the broad level, especially around resumes, interviews, LinkedIn, and generic career motivation. However, the landscape is less coherent around the lived experience of young adults who are confused, under-networked, and unsure how professional workplaces actually work.

The opportunity is not to compete as another generic career advice channel. The stronger opportunity is to become a plain-English workplace translator and practical mentor: someone who helps young adults choose a direction, tell their story, ask for help, and navigate the first stages of professional life.

## Method

This example assumes a directional sample across direct, problem-based, task-based, and adjacent YouTube search phrases. Results would be deduplicated, grouped into clusters, and scored for supply, demand, quality gap, freshness, distinctiveness, and client fit.

## Top landscape read

| Cluster | Read |
|---|---|
| Resume tips | Crowded and tactical |
| LinkedIn tips | Crowded and platform-specific |
| Interview prep | Crowded but still high demand |
| Career confusion | High emotional demand, mixed quality |
| Mentorship | Often generic or sales-oriented |
| Workplace navigation | Less saturated and potentially distinctive |
| First corporate job | Practical, relatable, underdeveloped |
| Career story / messy experience | Strong opportunity if handled well |

## Recommended positioning

> Plain-English career mentoring for young adults who are overwhelmed by vague advice, unsure how workplaces actually work, and need help translating messy experience into a credible next step.

## Why this is distinctive

Most career content answers narrow tactical questions: resume, interview, LinkedIn, application strategy. Those topics matter, but they often assume the viewer already understands what they want and how professional environments work.

The gap is the middle layer:

- How do I choose a direction?
- How do I explain myself?
- How do I ask for help?
- How do I know if a role is a fit?
- How do I communicate with a manager?
- How do I avoid sounding inexperienced?
- How do I recover from mistakes?
- How do I build confidence without pretending?
