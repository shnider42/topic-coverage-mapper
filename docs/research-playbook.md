# Research Playbook

## Objective

Map existing YouTube coverage for a topic and identify distinctive opportunities for a client.

The goal is not a perfect count. The goal is a decision-useful landscape scan.

## Research questions

1. What phrases do people use when searching for this topic?
2. Which channels and videos dominate those phrases?
3. What content formats are most common?
4. Which audience segments are served well?
5. Which audience segments are underserved?
6. Which advice is repeated so often that it has become commodity content?
7. Which topics receive high engagement but weak explanation?
8. Which gaps could become a differentiated offer, content series, or service package?

## Phase 1: Define the topic

Write a one-paragraph topic boundary.

Example:

> Young adult career coaching / mentoring includes advice for people roughly 18–30 who are choosing a career path, entering the workforce, changing early jobs, recovering from early-career uncertainty, building resumes, learning workplace norms, interviewing, networking, or translating messy experience into a coherent story.

## Phase 2: Build a query map

Use 4 categories:

### Direct terms

- young adult career coaching
- career mentor for young adults
- career advice for young adults

### Problem terms

- I don't know what career I want
- how to choose a career path
- what should I do after college
- career anxiety after college

### Task terms

- first resume after college
- first job interview tips
- LinkedIn tips for college graduates
- networking after college

### Adjacent terms

- quarter life crisis career
- career change in your 20s
- life after college advice
- entry level job advice

## Phase 3: Sample YouTube

For a first pass:

- 10–20 queries
- 25–50 results per query
- 200–800 raw rows before dedupe

For a deeper pass:

- 30–60 queries
- 50 results per query
- channel-level review
- comment sampling
- publish date and freshness review
- Google Trends comparison

## Phase 4: Capture metadata

Use `data/spreadsheet_template.csv`.

Minimum fields:

- query
- platform
- rank
- title
- channel
- url
- publish_date
- view_count
- video_length
- audience
- content_angle
- format
- cluster
- quality_notes
- gap_notes
- differentiation_notes

## Phase 5: Cluster the landscape

Example clusters for young adult career coaching:

1. Generic career advice
2. Resume and LinkedIn optimization
3. Interview preparation
4. Recruiter advice
5. College graduate transition
6. Career anxiety / quarter-life crisis
7. Career change in your 20s
8. Personal branding / creator hustle
9. Mentorship and coaching sales content
10. Workplace navigation and soft skills

## Phase 6: Score clusters

Use the rubric in `coverage-scoring-rubric.md`.

Prioritize clusters where:

- demand is visible
- current supply is repetitive
- advice is generic
- freshness is weak
- the client has a credible lived-experience angle

## Phase 7: Turn gaps into strategy

For each gap, write:

- What the existing content says
- What it misses
- Why the audience still has pain
- What the client could say differently
- What content format could prove the difference
- What offer or service could emerge

## Phase 8: Present the client memo

Use `client-brief-template.md`.

Keep it clear, specific, and honest about methodology limits.

## Important caveats

- YouTube search is not a full platform census.
- Search results can vary by time, location, account, language, and ranking changes.
- API sampling can miss videos that manual browsing surfaces.
- High view count does not always mean high relevance.
- Low view count does not always mean low value.
- The goal is useful directional insight, not academic precision.
