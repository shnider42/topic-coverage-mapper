## 9. Gaps and opportunities

| Gap | Why it matters | Possible client angle |
|---|---|---|
| Generic advice | audience needs specifics | practical mentor approach |
| Resume-only framing | job search is not the whole problem | career story and workplace navigation |
| Lack of lived experience | advice feels abstract | grounded workplace translator |
| Missing soft-skills guidance | young adults need scripts | first-job survival series |

## 10. Recommended positioning

Write a direct positioning recommendation.

Example:

> The strongest opportunity is not to become another generic career advice channel. The stronger path is to become a practical workplace translator for young adults: plain-English guidance on choosing a direction, telling a credible career story, finding mentorship, and navigating the first stages of professional work.

## 12. Caveats

- This is a directional scan, not a complete YouTube census.
- Search results may shift over time.
- View counts are demand proxies, not proof of conversion.
- Manual review is needed to assess quality.
- Additional research could include comments, Google Trends, TikTok, Reddit, LinkedIn, and competitor websites.

## 13. Next steps

1. Validate search language with the client.
2. Run a first-pass sample.
3. Score clusters.
4. Choose 3–5 content wedges.
5. Produce test content or offer copy.
6. Review performance and refine.
