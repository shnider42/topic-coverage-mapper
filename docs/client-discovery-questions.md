# Client Discovery Questions

Use these questions before or during the landscape scan.

## Business context

1. What is the client trying to decide?
2. Is the goal content strategy, offer design, competitor research, or market validation?
3. Who is the intended audience?
4. What would count as a useful answer?
5. Is this for a one-time report, ongoing monitoring, or a repeatable service?

## Topic definition

1. What is the exact topic?
2. What adjacent topics should be included?
3. What topics should be excluded?
4. Are there specific phrases the client already uses?
5. Are there phrases the audience likely uses instead?

## Audience

1. Who is the target viewer or buyer?
2. What age range, life stage, or role?
3. What problem are they trying to solve?
4. What would they type into YouTube when they are confused?
5. What would they type when they are ready to buy?

## Competitive landscape

1. Who does the client already think is a competitor?
2. Are there known creators, coaches, companies, or channels?
3. Are there non-YouTube competitors?
4. Is the client trying to avoid sounding like anyone?
5. Is there a benchmark channel they admire?

## Differentiation

1. What can the client credibly say that others cannot?
2. What lived experience does the client bring?
3. What tone should the client avoid?
4. What should the client be known for?
5. What content would feel authentic?

## Deliverable

1. Does the client need a report, deck, spreadsheet, or recommendation memo?
2. How much detail is useful?
3. Is this for internal strategy or external presentation?
4. What decision will be made after the research?
5. What is the deadline?
